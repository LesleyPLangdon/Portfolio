ABOUT ME

I am Lesley Langdon, wife, mother, self-trained chef, weekend horticulturist, and cum laude graduate pursuing a new career in computer science and a MBA while dreaming of hiking the Appalachian Trail one day. After careers as a restaurant manager and realtor I would like to combine my new skills with my existing tallents to fulfill my passion for helping others find success. I am actively looking for employment as a data analyst, project manager, or small business consultant.

I achieved the position of general manager for Chili's restaurants after climbing the ranks from line cook up through management. I was the general manager of a culinary test restaurant well as a management training location. I was trusted with our newest menu items and our newest managers, responsibilities that I took very seriously. The way that I ran my restaurant affected not only my staff and our guests but also the staff and guests of almost 20 other restaurants and the careers of up to a dozen managers a year. From my time as a restaurant manager, I learned leadership, organization, managing and understanding budgets, and to take responsibility for everything that happened under my charge. As rewarding as my career as a restaurant manager was, I always knew that it was not the career I wanted to eventually retire from.

About 10 years ago I made the leap, left the security of a salary position as a restaurant manager and dove headfirst into real estate. It was exciting. Every deal is different. At times I helped people make the single most significant purchase of their lives, other times I took on the responsibility of ensuring that a seller got the most they could out of the sale of their home. Selling real estate is a tremendous responsibility. I learned to negotiate and to work with different types of people. Possibly most valuable, I learned to really listen to what my client wanted and what they needed. I was working in real estate at an exciting time. Technology was constantly changing the way we marketed properties and the way we conducted business. Over time, I became the go to person for technology questions. Whether it was a problem with their personal computer, help setting up their social media or guidance managing their website I would do whatever I could to help out the other agents in my company. That was the start of what would become my pursuit of a career in technology.

I continued to work as a realtor while I began pursuing my computer science degree. After my daughter was born, I reduced my hours a bit, only working with my investors and other repeat clients so I could keep my daughter with me. I felt so blessed to be in an industry that afforded me the opportunity to juggle it all. That became a more difficult task as my daughter got older. She has a medical condition that made us uncomfortable sending her to daycare when she was so young. In 2019 I continued to reduce my work hours and eventually left real estate to focus on my education and my daughter.

Fast forward to 2021, I have completed my BS in computer science, began working towards my MBA, my daughter is ready for school, and I am excited to get to work. Feel free to browse around my site. You will find the history of my personal mission statement and how it guides me forward. I have included some sample projects including this website. If you would like to get in touch check out my contact page.
